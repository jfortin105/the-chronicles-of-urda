#Mechanics-Rules 
## Characters and Character Sheets
###### Stats:
- Standard array ---> roll 3x 3d6 and assign as you wish
###### Expanded Background tables:
- Standard Shadowdark background table will be expanded with backgrounds from other supplements and concepts that fit the world
###### Motivations:
- What drives your character? What are they trying to accomplish? What do they want?
- Motivations are an additional character attribute to determine when creating your character and are included to help frame the perspective of your character and the roleplaying aspect of play.
- A [[Mechanics and Rules/Random Table - Motivations\|Random Table - Motivations]] is provided if you would like a random/procedural motivation for your character.
###### Experiences:
- Experiences are an additional mechanical component of your character/character sheet that are designed to fill the same niche as 5e skills, but with less rigidity, and provide 
- Experiences are a specific words or phrases that:
	- inform the character concept.
	- embodies something distinctive about your character
	- details a set of skills your character has acquired in their past
	- sum up key parts of your character's background or expertise
- Experiences are use in gameplay as a bonus modifier that can be applied to specific dice rolls that are related to one of your experiences (per DM's adjudication).
	- Applying an experience grants a +2 modifier to related dice roll
- Experiences can't give you spells or specific game abilities nor should they be too mechanically-oriented. 

| Character Level | Experiences |
| --------------- | ----------- |
| 1               | 2           |
| 3               | 3           |
| 5               | 4           |
| 8               | 5           |
| 10              | 6           |
- Each character starts with 2 experiences and gains additional experiences as play progresses and levels are acquired. 
	- Experiences gained as you level up should reflective narrative moments/heroic moments/pivot moments from adventuring.
###### Starting HP:
- At level 1, your starting HP is equal to the maximum value of your classes hit die. Do not roll or add your constitution modifier.
###### Increasing HP:
- When you level up, roll all of your hit dice (including from your new level) and calculate the total. If this total exceeds your current HP value, then the rolled total becomes your new HP value.
- If the rolled total does not exceed your current HP value, then add 1 to your current HP.
###### Alignment:
- Traditional 9-point alignment will replace Shadowdark's 3-point alignment.
## Combat

###### Monster HP visibility
- Damage dealt to opponents will be counted up from zero and made known information to all players at all times
###### Natural 1's (AKA fumbles)
- Rolling a natural 1 with a material weapon will impose a -1 damage penalty to that weapon. These penalties cannot total negate a weapons damage potential and cap at a minimum damage value of 1.
